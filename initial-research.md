# Dander — Initial Research

**Date:** 2026-03-12

## Concept

"Strava for walking, but optimized for enjoyment not fitness" — AI-generated scenic routes, discovery of interesting spots, aesthetic walk scoring, gamification around exploration.

The name "Dander" comes from Irish/Northern Irish slang for a leisurely walk or stroll.

---

## Direct Competitors

| App | What It Does | Traction | Gap vs Dander |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Go Jauntly** | UK walking app. Hand-curated nature walks, basic challenges | 450K downloads, 40K MAU, £500K revenue. TfL/Boots partnerships | No AI route generation. No scoring. No real gamification. Routes are hand-curated, not personalized |
| **Walkable** | Fog-of-war map — walk to "uncover" buildings | 19 reviews, 3.8 stars. Tiny. iOS only | No route suggestions. No aesthetic scoring. Just tracks where you've been. Buggy |
| **Fog of World** | OG fog-of-war tracker (since 2012) | Niche but persistent | Pure tracker. No route generation, no scoring, no discovery |
| **RouteAI** | AI-generated routes with "scenic" option | Small/new | Running-focused. No scoring, no gamification, no discovery layer |
| **WayGenAI** | Natural language route planning ("find me a hidden beach") | New | Travel/tourism focused, not daily walking. No gamification |
| **Walkonomics** | Rates walkability of streets (safety, trees, beauty) | Academic/urban planning tool | Not a consumer app. Rates infrastructure, not experience |
| **Komoot** | Hiking route planner with user highlights and photos | Large (hiking market) | Fitness/hiking oriented. Not about urban walking enjoyment |

## Gamified Walking (Adjacent)

| App | What It Does | Traction | Gap vs Dander |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pokemon Go** | AR creature collection while walking | **$8B+ lifetime revenue** | Walking is incidental — it's about Pokemon, not the route or the place |
| **Pikmin Bloom** | Walk to grow flower gardens | Modest (Niantic's smaller title) | Walking is a mechanic, not the experience |
| **MysteryHike** | City scavenger hunts, XP, leaderboards | Small, tourism-focused | Tourist activity, not daily routine |

| **Walk the World** | Virtual global adventures via daily steps | New (launched Aug 2025) | Fantasy/virtual — you're "walking" the Champs-Élysées from your suburb |

## Fitness Walking (Not Competitors)

Strava, Map My Walk, Apple Fitness — all track **distance/pace/calories**. None care about route beauty or enjoyment. As one review site put it: *"Using Strava for walking is like using a racing bike for grocery shopping — technically possible, but missing the point."*

---

## The Gap

**Nobody combines these three things:**

1. **AI-generated routes optimized for enjoyment** — "give me a 30-min walk with greenery, coffee shops, and no busy roads"
2. **Aesthetic scoring** — rate/score the beauty and enjoyment of your walks
3. **Discovery gamification** — XP for finding new spots, streaks, challenges, "unlock" your neighbourhood

Go Jauntly does curated walks (no AI, no scoring). Walkable does fog gamification (no routes, no scoring). RouteAI does AI routes (no scoring, no gamification). **Nobody does all three.**

---

## Market Tailwinds

- Walking is the #1 physical activity globally, growing post-COVID
- "Romanticize your life" is a massive ongoing TikTok trend
- Strava explicitly underserves walkers (multiple articles calling this out)
- Pokemon Go proved gamified walking is an **$8 billion** market
- AI route generation is now technically feasible (wasn't 2 years ago)
- Go Jauntly proves UK walking apps can get partnerships (TfL, Boots, local authorities) — but at only 450K downloads, the market is wide open

## Shareability / Viral Loop

- Walk scorecards ("My morning dander scored 87/100") — screenshot-friendly
- "I discovered 14 hidden gems in my neighbourhood" — TikTok content
- Leaderboards and streaks with friends
- Before/after neighbourhood coverage maps (fog-of-war reveal)
- Beautiful route maps overlaid on satellite imagery

## Monetisation

- **Freemium**: free basic tracking + 1 AI route/day. Premium ($4.99/mo) for unlimited AI routes, detailed scoring, advanced gamification
- **Local business partnerships**: cafes/shops pay to be "featured stops" on routes
- **Council/tourism partnerships**: Go Jauntly already proved this works in the UK
- **Data**: anonymised walking pattern data valuable to urban planners (like Strava Metro)

---

## Confidence Rating: HIGH (8/10)

The specific product Dander would be genuinely doesn't exist. The closest competitor (Go Jauntly) has only 450K downloads and £500K total revenue in years — suggesting the market is underserved, not dead. The combination of AI route generation + enjoyment scoring + gamification is novel. Market timing is excellent.

**Risk factors (keeping it from 10/10):**
- Route quality depends on underlying map/POI data (Google Places API costs, OpenStreetMap coverage)
- Go Jauntly could add AI features (they already mentioned AI in a 2020 TechCrunch article)
- Building truly good "scenic" route scoring requires training data that doesn't exist yet
